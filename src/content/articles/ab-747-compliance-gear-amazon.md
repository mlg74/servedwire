## The Core Requirement (Quick Refresher)

Starting January 1, 2027, every process service attempt in California must be documented with a photograph that contains:

- **GPS coordinates** embedded in the image metadata
- **A timestamp** reflecting when the photo was taken

Your phone technically does this already — *if* you have location services enabled for the camera app. The problem is that modern phones strip GPS metadata from photos when you share or email them, and many attorneys and courts want more than a phone photo.

The gear below gives you more reliable, more defensible documentation.

## What You Can Skip

A few things that get marketed at servers that aren't worth the spend:

**Dedicated GPS loggers (standalone units):** These track your route but don't photograph anything. For AB 747, you need the GPS in the photo metadata, not a separate track log. Skip it.

**GoPro without GPS:** Many older GoPro models don't have built-in GPS. Check before you buy — the Hero 11 and earlier standard editions require an add-on. Get a model with GPS built in or use something else.

**Print-your-own GPS overlays:** Some apps let you "stamp" a GPS coordinate on a photo after the fact. Don't rely on these for compliance. Courts and attorneys are going to want clean EXIF metadata, not a text overlay that could be faked.

## The Minimum Viable Compliance Kit

If you want the absolute cheapest path to AB 747 compliance, here it is:

1. **Your current smartphone** — with location services enabled for the camera
2. **A process serving app** that captures and stores GPS metadata server-side (ServeManager, ABC Legal 24L, ProofServe)
3. **A 20,000 mAh battery pack** so you don't run dry mid-day
