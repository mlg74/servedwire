# AB 747 GPS Compliance: 5 Field Apps Already Delivering Court-Ready Proofs in 2026

January 1, 2027 is the hard deadline. But the process servers who will sail through it aren't the ones who'll scramble in December — they're the ones who started running compliant documentation six months ago.

The good news: the tools are already here. You don't need to wait for industry-specific software to catch up with AB 747. Multiple apps are already producing GPS-verified, timestamped photo documentation that holds up in court. Some are built specifically for process servers; others are general field documentation tools that the industry has quietly adopted.

Here's what's actually working in the field in 2026, and how each option fits different kinds of servers.

---

## What "Court-Ready" Actually Means

Before the list, a definition. For a GPS photo to be legally defensible under AB 747, it needs to accomplish a few things:

1. **GPS coordinates embedded in EXIF metadata** — the location data is baked into the file, not just typed into a note
2. **An accurate timestamp** tied to the moment the photo was taken, not added later
3. **Visible address context** — enough in the frame to identify the property
4. **Audit-trail integrity** — the file hasn't been modified after capture

The apps below meet all four criteria when used correctly. Which one is right for you depends on your volume, your existing workflow, and how much integration you want between documentation and case management.

## 1. Timestamp Camera Pro

**Best for: Solo servers who want a simple, phone-based solution**

[Timestamp Camera Pro](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=timestamp+camera+gps+app&tag=servedwire-20) is the most widely adopted standalone documentation app among California process servers right now. It's available on both iPhone and Android, and it does exactly what the name says: takes photos with the timestamp and GPS coordinates burned visibly onto the image itself.

That last part matters more than it sounds. When you burn data directly onto the photo — not just into the EXIF metadata — you eliminate the objection that metadata is easy to spoof or strip. What's on the image is what anyone with eyes can see.

**What it does well:**
- Overlays GPS coordinates, reverse-geocoded address, date, and time directly onto every photo
- Writes EXIF metadata in parallel with the visible overlay
- Works offline — GPS capture doesn't require data connectivity
- Extremely simple to use: open, point, shoot

**The tradeoff:**
It's a photo app, not a case management system. You'll need to organize your photos by case manually, or use a naming convention that lets you match photos to proofs of service. For a server doing 5–10 cases a week, that's manageable. For someone running 50 cases at a time, you'll want something that integrates deeper.

**Pricing:** Under $5 one-time on the App Store and Google Play. Easily the lowest barrier to entry on this list.

## 2. GPS Map Camera

**Best for: Android users who want a free starting point**

[GPS Map Camera](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=gps+camera+field+documentation&tag=servedwire-20) is the Android-native cousin of Timestamp Camera Pro. It overlays GPS coordinates and a map thumbnail directly onto photos — giving you not just the coordinates but a visual confirmation that the coordinates match a real location.

The map thumbnail is an underrated feature for legal documentation. It's hard for opposing counsel to argue that your coordinates were fabricated when the photo shows a recognizable map pin at the address in question.

**What it does well:**
- Map thumbnail embedded in the photo gives visual geographic confirmation
- Coordinate and timestamp overlay
- No ongoing subscription

**The tradeoff:**
Like Timestamp Camera Pro, this is documentation-only. It doesn't connect to your case management. Also Android-only — iPhone users should look at Timestamp Camera Pro or the options below.

**Pricing:** Free with ads; paid version available for a few dollars.

## 3. ServeManager

**Best for: Full-service process servers who want documentation and case management in one place**

[ServeManager](https://www.servemanager.com) is the most established purpose-built software platform in the process serving industry. It handles job assignment, tracking, invoicing, and proof-of-service generation — and its mobile app has GPS photo capture integrated directly into the job workflow.

The difference from the photo-only apps above: when you take a GPS photo in ServeManager, it's automatically attached to the right case, with the attempt number and timestamp logged against that job record. There's no "file this photo in the right folder" step. The documentation is organizational as well as legal.

**What it does well:**
- GPS-documented attempts tied directly to case records
- Proof of service generation pulls from logged attempt data
- Attorney client access — some servers give attorneys a login to track job status
- Scales from solo servers to multi-person agencies

**The tradeoff:**
It's a subscription platform. If you're a very low-volume server, the monthly cost may not pencil out against simpler alternatives. Also, some servers report that the mobile app interface, while functional, isn't as polished as a consumer photo app.

**Pricing:** Subscription-based; pricing varies by plan and volume. Check servemanager.com for current rates — they adjust periodically.

## 5. ProofServe

**Best for: Servers specifically building for AB 747 compliance documentation**

ProofServe is a newer entrant in the process serving tech space, designed with GPS compliance documentation as its primary purpose. Unlike the general-purpose photo apps, it's built around the specific documentation chain that AB 747 requires: each attempt is logged as part of a serve record, photos are GPS-tagged and stored with the case, and the platform generates serve-attempt documentation formatted for proof of service use.

Because it's newer, the user community is smaller and the third-party integrations are more limited than ServeManager. But for servers who want a purpose-built tool focused specifically on the documentation layer — without the full case management overhead — it's worth evaluating.

**What it does well:**
- Built specifically for AB 747-style documentation workflows
- GPS photo capture tied to serve attempt records
- Attempt-by-attempt documentation trail

**The tradeoff:**
Less established than ServeManager; smaller user base means fewer peer reviews and community resources to draw on. Verify current features and pricing directly with the provider, as the platform is actively evolving.

**Pricing:** Subscription; check proofserve.com for current rates.

Match the tool to your actual volume and workflow — not to what sounds most impressive.

Build the habit now. Tap the app before you tap the door.
